GRADE 8 ECCLESIASTES ------ ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS

Essential elements and compounds are crucial for the survival and function of living organisms. In this section, we will explore the essential elements and compounds which are vital for maintaining and supporting life functions.

ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS

  1. Carbon(C): The backbone of organic molecules, including proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, and lipids.
  2. Hydrogen(H): A component of water and organic molecules, essential for energy production and metabolism.
  3. Oxygen(O): Needed for cellular respiration and is a part of water and organic molecules.
  4. Nitrogen(N): A key component of amino acids (the building blocks of proteins) and nucleic acids (DNA and RNA).
  5. Phosphorus(P): Important for energy transfer (ATP), nucleic acids, and phospholipids in cell membranes.
  6. Sulfur(S): Found in some amino acids and vitamins, contributing to protein structure and function.
  7. Calcium(Ca): Vital for cell signaling, muscle contraction, and bone and teeth structure.
  8. Potassium(K): Regulates cell function and fluid balance, and is crucial for nerve and muscle function.
  9. Sodium(Na): Important for maintaining fluid balance, nerve function, and muscle contraction.
  10. Magnesium(Mg): Acts as a cofactor for many enzymes and is important for muscle and nerve function.

These elements and compounds work together to support life processes, including metabolism, growth, and reproduction
