WHAT IS WATER?

Water is chemical compund made of the elements of hydrogen and oxygen. This compound helps support life in many ways. Animals and plants use water as a source of mineral or nutrition. Water can be solid, liquid, or gas.

How does water affect our environment?

Less Water Affects the Environment

For example, lower water levels can contribute to higher concentrations of natural and human pollutants. Less water going down the drain means more water available in the lakes, rivers and streams that we use for recreation and wildlife uses to survive.

Water for the environment supports the health of the river so that it can in turn provide for human needs. As water moves onto the floodplain, it releases carbon that energises the food web. River pulses trigger fish breeding and movement.

Water has a profound impact on our environment in several ways:

  1. Ecosystems
    • Water is essential for the survival of plants and animals. It supports aquatic ecosystems like rivers, lakes, and oceans, and terrestrial ecosystems by sustaining vegetation.
  2. Climate Regulation
    • Water bodies influence weather patterns and climate. For instance, large bodies of water like oceans and seas help regulate temperatures by storing and distributing heat.
  3. Weather Patterns
    • The evaporation of water from the surface contributes to cloud formation and precipitation, affecting weather and climate systems globally.
  4. Soil Fertility
    • Water helps in the weathering of rocks and the transportation of minerals, which enhances soil fertility and supports agriculture.
  5. Human Activities
    • Water is crucial for drinking, agriculture, industry, and energy production. However, human activities can lead to water pollution, which negatively affects both ecosystems and human health.
  6. Natural Disasters
    • Water-related phenomena, such as floods, droughts, and hurricanes, can have significant impacts on the environment and human settlements.

Overall, water plays a key role in shaping environmental conditions and supporting life on Earth.

How water affects our bodies:

  1. Hydration and Tissue Protection:
    • Water lubricates our tissues and keeps our joints cushioned.
  2. Waste Removal:
    • It helps our kidneys filter waste and prevents constipation.
  3. Digestion:
    • Water assists in breaking down food and absorbing nutrients.
  4. Temperature Regulation:
    • Sweat helps cool us down, preventing overheating.
  5. Cardiovascular Health:
    • Water is crucial for maintaining blood volume and pressure.
  6. Brain Function:
    • Dehydration can impair memory, concentration, and mood.
  7. Physical Performance:
    • Staying hydrated is essential for peak performance during exercise.
  8. Weight Management:
    • Water can help control appetite and aid in weight loss.
  9. Skin Health:
    • It keeps our skin hydrated and may promote collagen production.
  10. Immune System Support:
    • Water helps our bodies fight off infections and diseases.

Remember to drink enough water daily to stay hydrated and maintain optimal health. Men should aim for about 15.5 cups, and women 11.5 cups of fluids per day

WATER CYCLE

The water cycle describes how water helps in cooling down the Earth. It also shows the continuous movement and cycle of water between the Earth and its atmosphere. The water cycle has 7 stages

  • Evaporation
  • Condensation
  • Precipitation
  • Sublimation
  • Transpiration
  • Runoff
  • Infiltration

These stages can be seen through our everyday life like how evaporation can be seen while we boil water and how Precipitation is seen as rain. The main stages of the water cycle are Evaporation, Condensation, and lastly Precipitation.

IMPORTANCE AND PURPOSE OF THE WATER CYCLE:

The water cycle is important for it regulates the weather patterns here on Earth. It is also to spread the source of water so it can be available to animals, humans, insects, and more living organisms.

It is a provider, supporter, and maintainer to the richness of our land. It spreads resources such as water to give and support life here on Earth. It is a cycle that continues for generations. This is why it is important to take care of our home planet. If we ignore taking care of our home, the water cycle will be one of the things in our environment that will be affected by any harm we do.

THE 7 STAGES OF WATER CYCLE:

Evaporation

  • Evaporation is the process wherein a liquid turns into a gaseous state.
  • Water turns into the state og gas whenever the sun heats up any source of water.
Purpose: This stage is use by experts to separate the solute (solid) from the solvent(liquid). Example:

Boiling water, Drying clothes in the sun, Drying wet hair, etc…

Condensation

  • Condensation is the process where water vapor becomes liquid.
  • This is the reverse action of evaporation.
  • The water vapor will likely turn into cloud or a gas that is full of moisture.
  • (Dew is simply condensed water in the atmosphere.)
  • Air temperatures can reach or fall below the dew point naturally.
  • That is why ground, structures, and objects outside are usually coated with water droplets in the morning.
Purpose:
  • Condensation is responsible for making clouds. Without this stage, The liquid (i.e water) will remain in its gas state.
    • Example:

    Fog, Clouds, and more…

    Precipitation

    • Precipitation is a stage of the water cycle where a liquid or frozen water falls down to the Earth.
    • When water vapor condenses into bigger and bigger droplets of water.
    • When the drops are heavy enough, they fall to the earth.
    Purpose:
    • Precipitation cleans the atmosphere and wash out the air pollution and natural aerosols.
    • But mainly, it is to spread water supply to animals and humans.
    Example:

    Rain, hail, snow, and etc…

    Sublimation

    • Sublimation is where moisture enters the atmosphere directly from ice to snow.
    • Sublimation is a process where ice or snow directly turns into a gaseous state.
    Example:

    dry ice and solid fresheners

    Transpiration

    • Transpiration is the biological process by which water is lost in the form of water vapor from the aerial parts of the plants.
    Example:

    stomatal transpiration, cuticular transpiration, and lenticular transpiration

    Runoff

    • Runoff is when the precipitation is not soaked by the lands of the Earth but instantly moves into a nearby water source like streams or lakes.
    Purpose: It is to prevent moisture to build up and infiltration. Example: snowmelts

    Infiltration

    • Infiltration is a process where the water that came from the Precipitation stage falls down and is absorbed into the ground.
      • Example:

      pouring water into a sponge

      (These are all 7 stages of the water cycle)
